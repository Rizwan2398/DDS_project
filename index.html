Depression Causes

  • Biochemistry: Differences in certain chemicals / neurotransmitters in
    the brain may contribute to symptoms of depression.
  • Genetics: Depression can run in families. For example, a twin has a 70
    percent chance of having the illness sometime in life if the other
    identical twin has depression.
  • Personality: People who are easily overwhelmed by stress, or who are
    generally pessimistic, or have low self-esteem appear to be more likely
    to experience depression.
  • Environmental Factors: Continuous exposure to violence, abuse,
    neglect or poverty may make some people more vulnerable to
    depression.

Depression Symptoms

  • Feeling sad or having a depressed mood
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed
  • Changes in appetite — weight loss or gain unrelated to dieting
  • Trouble sleeping or sleeping too much

Depression Treatement

Depression is a treatable mental
illness. Components to the
management of depression:

  • Support: Ranging from discussing practical solutions and
    contributing stressors, to educating family members.
  • Psychotherapy also known as talk therapy, provided by clinical
    psychologists, such as cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT).
  • Medication: Specifically antidepressants are used for Depression.
